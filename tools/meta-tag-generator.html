Frequently Asked Questions

Meta tags are snippets of HTML code in the head section of your web pages that provide information about your page to search engines and website visitors. They're important for SEO because they help search engines understand what your page is about, which can improve your search rankings. Meta tags also control how your page appears when shared on social media platforms like Facebook and Twitter.

For meta titles, the recommended length is between 50-60 characters. Google typically displays the first 50-60 characters of a title tag, so keeping within this limit ensures your full title appears in search results. For meta descriptions, aim for 150-160 characters. While Google doesn't use meta descriptions as a direct ranking factor, a well-written description can improve click-through rates from search results.

Open Graph (OG) tags were developed by Facebook to control how content appears when shared on Facebook and other social media platforms. They define elements like the title, description, and featured image. Twitter Cards are similar but specific to Twitter, controlling how your content looks when shared on the platform. Both types of tags help create more engaging social media posts with rich previews rather than plain links.

Meta keywords have largely lost their importance for major search engines like Google, which confirmed they no longer use the meta keywords tag for ranking. However, some smaller search engines might still consider them, and they can be useful for internal site search functionality. While not harmful to include, it's better to focus your SEO efforts on quality content, proper meta titles, and descriptions rather than keyword tags.
